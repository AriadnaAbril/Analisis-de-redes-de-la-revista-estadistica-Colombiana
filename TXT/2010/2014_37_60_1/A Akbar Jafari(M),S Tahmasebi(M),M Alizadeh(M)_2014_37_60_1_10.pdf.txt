Abstract
In this paper, we introduce a new four-parameter generalized version of the Gompertz model which is called Beta-Gompertz (BG) distribution. It includes some well-known lifetime distributions such as Beta-exponential and generalized Gompertz distributions as special sub-models. This new distribution is quite ï¬‚exible and can be used eï¬€ectively in modeling survival data and reliability problems. It can have a decreasing, increasing, and bathtub-shaped failure rate function depending on its parameters. Some mathematical properties of the new distribution, such as closed-form expressions for the density, cumulative distribution, hazard rate function, the kth order moment, moment generating function, Shannon entropy, and the quantile measure are provided. We discuss maximum likelihood estimation of the BG parameters from one observed sample and derive the observed Fisherâ€™s information matrix. A simulation study is performed in order to investigate the properties of the proposed estimator. At the end, in order to show the BG distribution ï¬‚exibility, an application using a real data set is presented.
Key words: Beta generator, Gompertz distribution, Maximum likelihood estimation.

1. Introduction
    The Gompertz (G) distribution is a ï¬‚exible distribution that can be skewed to
the right and to the left. This distribution is a generalization of the exponential
(E) distribution and is commonly used in many applied problems, particularly
in lifetime data analysis (Johnson, Kotz & Balakrishnan 1995, p. 25). The G
distribution is considered for the analysis of survival, in some sciences such as
gerontology (Brown & Forbes 1974), computer (Ohishi, Okamura & Dohi 2009),
biology (Economos 1982), and marketing science (Bemmaor & Glady 2012). The
hazard rate function (hrf) of G distribution is an increasing function and often
applied to describe the distribution of adult life spans by actuaries and demogra-
phers (Willemse & Koppelaar 2000). The G distribution with parameters Î¸ > 0
and Î³ > 0 has the cumulative distribution function (cdf)

    In this section, we performed a simulation study in order to investigate the

erate 10,000 data sets with size n from the BG distribution with parameters a, b,
Î¸, and Î³, and compute the MLEâ€™s of the parameters. We assess the accuracy of the
approximation of the standard error of the MLEâ€™s determined through the Fisher

  iii. the standard errors of the MLEs decrease when the sample size increases.

the MLE are satisfactory.
